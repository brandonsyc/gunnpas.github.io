About Public Advocacy and Speech

Public Advocacy

  • Speak on an issue or cause you believe in
  • Present on radio, at events, in local competitions
  • Length: 3 – 7 minutes
  • Persuasive
  • Individual

Speech

  • Speak on an issue or cause you believe in or
  • Present an interpretation of an original or published speach or piece of literature
  • Compete in tournaments
  • Length: 7 – 10 minutes
  • Persuasive
  • Individual (except Duo Interp)

Debate

  • Debate a policy resolution
  • Do research prep
  • Compete in tournaments
  • Length: 40 – 60 minutes
  • Adversarial
  • Partnet (except LD)

Club Goals

Gain public speaking skills

Speak with confidence. Become more persuasive.

Advocate and Inspire

Participate in public forums and competitions. Have your voice and opinions heard.

Have fun

Share ideas. Practice together. Support each other's interests.

Choose an activity that interests you and think about how much time and effort you can put in. We can come up with a plan that works for you.
Student members work individually and independently. Fit the work in when you have time.
Participation can be one-time commitments.
Competitions and tournaments usually take 1 to 1.5 days. At tournaments, there is time in between rounds to do homework and hang out.

PA Forums and Competitions

Non-Profits

Lend your voice to non-profit organizations that support causes you believe in.
Acterra: work alongside environmental non-profit on clamate change issues.

Radio

Write and record an opinion piece and submit it to radio programs.
KQED Perspectives and Transom

Competitions

Compete in competitions based on an inspirational theme. Some offer scholarships.
Rotary, Lion's Club, Voice of Democracy

Speech Events

Students can choose to attend as many or as few as one tournament a year.
Students work individually on one piece to present. Depending on the event, the piece is either a published speech or something original the sutdent writes.
Students can use the same piece at all tournaments (except extemp).

Oratory

Original Oratory: Present an original persuasive speech on a topic of your choice.
Original Advocacy: Present an original persuasive speech identifying a problem and offer a legislative solution.

Interpretation

Humorous or Dramatic: Act out a cut down version of a published piece of literature without props or costumes.
Duo Interp: A HI or DI with two people.
Oratorical: Present a published speech that has been delivered in a public forum.

Extemporaneous

Choose from three given topics on current Domestic or International issues and in 30 minutes prepare a speech addressing the topic.
