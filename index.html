About Public Advocacy and Speech

In Public Advocacy and Speech, students personally choose a topic or cause in which they believe to present and speak. The goal is to persuade and inform others. The goal is to persuade and inform others. The foundation of our teaching will be based on competitive speech formats.

Gain public speaking skills. Speak with confidence. Become more persuasive.

Advocate and Inspire. Participate in public forums and competitions. Have your voice and opinions heard.

Have fun. Share ideas. Practice together. Support each other’s interests.

There is often confusion over speech versus debate. Both do require speaking skills. Speech, however, allows students to express their own views and opinions, supported by facts, evidence and personal experiences. In oratory and interpretation speech, students employ their creativity and incorporate elements of performance.

Speech is individually based (except duo) which means students can conveniently fit the work into their busy schedules, and the the duration of a speech is typically no more than 10 minutes long compared to a 40 minute debate round.

Public Advocacy

Students can participate in many different public forums and competitions that occur throughout the year. Students meet to share ideas and develop individual speeches.

Time commitment varies from presenting or competing in one event to working with local non-profits on a longer-term advocacy project.

Non-Profits

Lend your voice to non-profit organizations that support causes you believe in.

Radio

Write and record an opinion piece and submit it to radio programs.

Competitions

Compete in competitions based on an inspirational theme. Some offer scholarships.

Speech

Students can choose to attend as many or as few as one tournament a year. Although students practice together and give feedback at weekly meetings, the development work is done individually.

Typically, students create and present one piece. Depending on the event, the piece is either a published speech or something original that the student writes. The same piece can be presented at all tournaments.

Original

Write a 10 minute three-point original speech on any topic. Can be persuasive or advocative.

Interpretation

Cut down any published piece of literature into a 10 minute skit without props or costumes. Can be done individually or in a duo.

Extemporaneous

Every round, recieve a new topic and 30 minutes to prepare a 7 minute speech. Must be memorized and have three points.
